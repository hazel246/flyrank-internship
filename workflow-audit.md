# FL-01: AI Workflow Audit and Tool Setup

**Track:** AI Fluency · **Week 1**

> This audit maps my real recurring tasks and classifies how much AI should be
> involved in each, using the task-classification framework (just me / delegate
> with review / collaborate / fully automate). The goal is to know where AI
> genuinely helps, where it wastes time, and where it should never be trusted.

---

## 1. Workflow audit

Classification key:
- **Just me** : AI should not do this (my own learning, judgement, human connection, or the part that is my differentiator)
- **Delegate + review** : AI does the bulk, I check the output
- **Collaborate** : AI and I work together, back and forth, I stay in control
- **Automate** : AI does it with no review needed

| # | Recurring task | Classification | One-line rationale |
|---|----------------|----------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Writing app code (my edtech project) | Delegate + review | AI writes it, but I must understand and check every part before it ships |
| 2 | Designing API contracts | Collaborate | I design the structure with AI input, then make the final decisions myself |
| 3 | Extracting and classifying past-paper questions | Delegate + review | AI does the bulk extraction, I review the judgement calls |
| 4 | Writing teaching notes (explanations, common mistakes) | Just me | My teaching voice and the real mistakes I see in tutoring are my differentiator; AI makes it generic |
| 5 | Learning to code (daily practice problems) | Just me | If AI solves them, I do not learn; the whole point is building my own skill |
| 6 | Debugging errors in my code | Collaborate | I work through errors with AI guiding me, so I understand why they happened |
| 7 | Designing UI / homepage mockups | Collaborate | AI drafts options, I judge taste and direction |
| 8 | Tutoring my students | Just me | Reading a student in real time and human connection cannot be delegated |
| 9 | Planning my daily schedule | Just me | Depends on my energy and priorities; a personal judgement call |
| 10 | Writing SQL / database schema | Delegate + review | AI drafts it, I verify the structure matches what I actually need |
| 11 | Researching new backend/AI concepts | Collaborate | I learn best by questioning and discussing, not passive reading |
| 12 | Drafting original practice questions | Delegate + review | AI drafts from references, I review for quality and originality |
| 13 | Formatting notes into clean documents | Delegate + review | AI handles the layout, I check the content is right |
| 14 | Summarising long articles or docs | Delegate + review | AI summarises, I verify against the source before trusting it |
| 15 | Reflecting on my goals and progress | Just me | This is personal and shapes my decisions; it has to be my own thinking |

## 3. Three target tasks for FL-02 to FL-04

These are the three tasks I will reuse and improve across the next assignments,
with a clear definition of what "done well" means for each.

| Target task | What "done well" means (measurable) |
